PART A – BASIC SCIENCES (40 QUESTIONS)

ANATOMY (4)
1. Topic: Anatomy – Eye
Question: Which structure directly regulates the amount of light entering the eye?
(A) Cornea
(B) Lens
(C) Pupil
(D) Iris
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: The iris contains smooth muscle that contracts (parasympathetic) to constrict the pupil and dilates (sympathetic) to widen it. The pupil itself is the opening, not the regulator.

2. Topic: Anatomy – Middle ear
Question: The ossicle that transmits vibrations to the oval window is the:
(A) Malleus
(B) Incus
(C) Stapes
(D) Hyoid
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The stapes footplate fits into the oval window, transmitting mechanical energy to the cochlear fluid.

3. Topic: Anatomy – Brain
Question: Which lobe of the brain is primarily associated with auditory processing and memory?
(A) Frontal
(B) Temporal
(C) Parietal
(D) Occipital
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The temporal lobe houses the primary auditory cortex (Heschl's gyrus) and the hippocampus (memory).

4. Topic: Anatomy – Heart
Question: The coronary arteries originate from:
(A) Pulmonary trunk
(B) Ascending aorta
(C) Left ventricle
(D) Superior vena cava
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The right and left coronary arteries arise from the sinuses of Valsalva in the ascending aorta just above the aortic valve.

PHYSIOLOGY (5)
5. Topic: Physiology – Blood pressure
Question: Which of the following hormones directly causes vasoconstriction and increased blood pressure?
(A) Atrial natriuretic peptide
(B) Bradykinin
(C) Angiotensin II
(D) Prostacyclin
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Angiotensin II is a potent vasoconstrictor and stimulates aldosterone release, raising BP. ANP and bradykinin cause vasodilation.

6. Topic: Physiology – Respiration
Question: The majority of carbon dioxide is transported in the blood as:
(A) Dissolved CO2
(B) Carbaminohemoglobin
(C) Bicarbonate ions (HCO3-)
(D) Carbonic acid
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: About 70% of CO2 is transported as bicarbonate (formed in RBCs via carbonic anhydrase). Only 7% is dissolved, and 23% as carbaminohemoglobin.

7. Topic: Physiology – Renal
Question: Which part of the nephron is primarily impermeable to water in the absence of ADH?
(A) Proximal convoluted tubule
(B) Descending loop of Henle
(C) Ascending loop of Henle
(D) Collecting duct
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: The collecting duct is water‑impermeable without ADH; ADH inserts aquaporin‑2 channels to allow water reabsorption.

8. Topic: Physiology – Endocrine
Question: Which hormone is secreted in response to low blood calcium?
(A) Calcitonin
(B) Parathyroid hormone (PTH)
(C) Thyroid hormone
(D) Aldosterone
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: PTH increases serum calcium by bone resorption, renal reabsorption, and calcitriol activation. Calcitonin lowers calcium.

9. Topic: Physiology – Muscle contraction
Question: During isometric contraction, muscle length:
(A) Shortens
(B) Lengthens
(C) Remains constant while tension increases
(D) Oscillates
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Isometric: tension increases but muscle length does not change (e.g., holding a weight steady). Isotonic: length changes.

SOCIOLOGY (3)
10. Topic: Sociology – Social institutions
Question: The family is considered a primary group because it is characterized by:
(A) Large size and impersonal relationships
(B) Goal‑oriented and temporary interactions
(C) Intimate, face‑to‑face, and enduring relationships
(D) Formal rules and contractual obligations
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Primary groups (family, close friends) have intimate, long‑term, emotional bonds. Secondary groups are larger and impersonal.

11. Topic: Sociology – Culture
Question: The belief that one's own culture is superior to others is called:
(A) Cultural relativism
(B) Ethnocentrism
(C) Cultural diffusion
(D) Xenocentrism
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Ethnocentrism judges other cultures by the standards of one's own. Cultural relativism views cultures objectively.

12. Topic: Sociology – Social stratification
Question: In a closed system of stratification, social position is determined primarily by:
(A) Achieved status
(B) Ascribed status (birth)
(C) Wealth and income
(D) Education level
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Closed systems (caste, slavery) assign status at birth with little mobility. Open systems (class) allow movement.

PSYCHOLOGY (4)
13. Topic: Psychology – Memory
Question: Forgetting that occurs because new information interferes with recall of old information is termed:
(A) Retroactive interference
(B) Proactive interference
(C) Repression
(D) Decay
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Retroactive = new impairs old. Proactive = old impairs new. Repression is unconscious blocking.

14. Topic: Psychology – Defense mechanisms
Question: A person who unconsciously attributes their own unacceptable thoughts to someone else is using:
(A) Rationalization
(B) Displacement
(C) Projection
(D) Sublimation
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Projection: attributing one's own feelings/impulses to another person (e.g., "He hates me" when actually "I hate him").

15. Topic: Psychology – Emotion
Question: The James‑Lange theory of emotion proposes that:
(A) Emotion precedes physiological arousal
(B) Physiological arousal precedes emotion
(C) Cognitive appraisal determines emotion
(D) Emotion and arousal occur simultaneously
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: James‑Lange: we feel emotion because we perceive bodily changes (e.g., we feel sad because we cry). Cannon‑Bard: simultaneous.

16. Topic: Psychology – Developmental
Question: According to Piaget, a child in the concrete operational stage (7–11 years) can:
(A) Think abstractly and hypothetically
(B) Use logic but only with concrete objects
(C) Understand object permanence
(D) Engage in egocentric thinking
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Concrete operational: logical reasoning about tangible events; abstract reasoning emerges in formal operational stage.

PHARMACOLOGY (7)
17. Topic: Pharmacology – Anticoagulants
Question: The antidote for heparin overdose is:
(A) Vitamin K
(B) Protamine sulfate
(C) Naloxone
(D) Flumazenil
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Protamine sulfate is a basic protein that binds to acidic heparin, neutralizing it. Vitamin K reverses warfarin.

18. Topic: Pharmacology – Antidiabetics
Question: Which oral antidiabetic drug carries a risk of lactic acidosis, especially in renal impairment?
(A) Metformin
(B) Glipizide
(C) Pioglitazone
(D) Sitagliptin
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Metformin (biguanide) can cause lactic acidosis in patients with renal failure, liver disease, or hypoxia.

19. Topic: Pharmacology – Antibiotics
Question: A patient with a penicillin allergy is most likely to also have a reaction to:
(A) Vancomycin
(B) Cephalosporins
(C) Macrolides
(D) Tetracyclines
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: There is about 5‑10% cross‑sensitivity between penicillins and cephalosporins due to similar beta‑lactam ring.

20. Topic: Pharmacology – Antihypertensives
Question: Which class of antihypertensive drugs is contraindicated in pregnancy because it can cause fetal renal agenesis?
(A) Beta‑blockers
(B) ACE inhibitors
(C) Calcium channel blockers
(D) Diuretics
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: ACE inhibitors (and ARBs) are fetotoxic, causing oligohydramnios and renal tubular dysplasia in the second/third trimester.

21. Topic: Pharmacology – Local anesthetics
Question: Epinephrine is added to local anesthetics to:
(A) Reduce pain on injection
(B) Increase duration of anesthesia by vasoconstriction
(C) Prevent allergic reactions
(D) Increase systemic absorption
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Epinephrine causes vasoconstriction, slowing absorption of local anesthetic, prolonging effect, and reducing bleeding.

22. Topic: Pharmacology – Antipsychotics
Question: Which side effect of typical antipsychotics is characterized by a "rabbit syndrome" (fine, rapid perioral tremors)?
(A) Tardive dyskinesia
(B) Akathisia
(C) Dystonia
(D) Parkinsonism
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Rabbit syndrome is a variant of drug‑induced parkinsonism – fine tremor of perioral muscles. Tardive dyskinesia is choreoathetoid.

23. Topic: Pharmacology – Opioids
Question: Naloxone (Narcan) reverses opioid overdose by:
(A) Activating opioid receptors
(B) Blocking opioid receptors as a competitive antagonist
(C) Increasing opioid metabolism
(D) Inhibiting opioid absorption
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Naloxone is a pure competitive antagonist at mu opioid receptors, rapidly displacing opioids.

MICROBIOLOGY (6)
24. Topic: Microbiology – Sterilization
Question: Which method is used to sterilize heat‑sensitive liquids (e.g., vaccines, serum)?
(A) Autoclaving
(B) Dry heat
(C) Filtration
(D) Boiling
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Membrane filtration (0.22 micron) removes bacteria and spores without heat; used for heat‑labile solutions.

25. Topic: Microbiology – Bacterial toxins
Question: Exotoxin responsible for the characteristic "bulbar palsy" and descending paralysis in botulism is:
(A) Tetanospasmin
(B) Botulinum toxin
(C) Enterotoxin
(D) Lipopolysaccharide (endotoxin)
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Botulinum toxin (from C. botulinum) blocks ACh release at neuromuscular junctions, causing flaccid paralysis.

26. Topic: Microbiology – Immunity
Question: Which type of immunity is conferred by injection of preformed antibodies (e.g., rabies immunoglobulin)?
(A) Active natural
(B) Active artificial
(C) Passive natural
(D) Passive artificial
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Passive artificial immunity = injection of antibodies (human or animal) for immediate but short‑term protection.

27. Topic: Microbiology – Viruses
Question: The hepatitis virus that is a DNA virus is:
(A) Hepatitis A
(B) Hepatitis B
(C) Hepatitis C
(D) Hepatitis E
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: HBV is a hepadnavirus (DNA). HAV, HCV, HEV are RNA viruses.

28. Topic: Microbiology – Gram staining
Question: A Gram‑negative coccus that causes gonorrhea and appears as "kidney bean" diplococci is:
(A) Neisseria meningitidis
(B) Neisseria gonorrhoeae
(C) Moraxella catarrhalis
(D) Haemophilus influenzae
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: N. gonorrhoeae is Gram‑negative diplococcus, oxidase positive; N. meningitidis is similar but causes meningitis.

29. Topic: Microbiology – Hospital infection
Question: The most common source of nosocomial infection in a hospital is:
(A) Contaminated food
(B) Airborne dust
(C) Patient's own endogenous flora
(D) Unsterilized surgical instruments
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Endogenous flora (patient's own skin, gut, respiratory) is the most frequent source; exogenous sources are less common.

NUTRITION (4)
30. Topic: Nutrition – Vitamins
Question: The major storage form of vitamin A in the liver is:
(A) Retinol
(B) Retinal
(C) Retinoic acid
(D) Beta‑carotene
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Vitamin A is stored as retinyl esters in hepatic stellate cells; retinol is the alcohol form used in transport.

31. Topic: Nutrition – Minerals
Question: Zinc deficiency is most commonly associated with which clinical sign?
(A) Angular stomatitis
(B) Parotid enlargement
(C) Delayed wound healing and hypogeusia
(D) Night blindness
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Zinc is a cofactor for enzymes; deficiency causes impaired wound healing, altered taste (hypogeusia), and hair loss.

32. Topic: Nutrition – Protein energy malnutrition
Question: Marasmus differs from kwashiorkor in that marasmus primarily results from:
(A) Protein deficiency with adequate calories
(B) Calorie (energy) deficiency
(C) Vitamin B12 deficiency
(D) Iron deficiency
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Marasmus = chronic energy deficit (wasting, no edema). Kwashiorkor = protein deficiency (edema, fatty liver).

33. Topic: Nutrition – BMR
Question: Which factor increases basal metabolic rate (BMR) the most?
(A) Starvation
(B) Hypothyroidism
(C) Fever (1°C increase)
(D) Old age
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Fever raises BMR by about 13% per °C. Hyperthyroidism and pregnancy also increase; starvation decreases.

PATHOLOGY (7)
34. Topic: Pathology – Inflammation
Question: Which of the following is a characteristic of chronic inflammation?
(A) Neutrophils as the predominant cell
(B) Immediate onset after injury
(C) Formation of granulomas
(D) Resolution within 24 hours
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Chronic inflammation is prolonged, with lymphocytes, macrophages, plasma cells, and sometimes granulomas.

35. Topic: Pathology – Thrombosis
Question: The three components of Virchow's triad are:
(A) Endothelial injury, hypercoagulability, stasis
(B) Hypoxia, acidosis, infection
(C) Atherosclerosis, embolus, infarction
(D) Vasospasm, platelet plug, fibrin mesh
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Virchow's triad: endothelial damage, abnormal blood flow (stasis/turbulence), and hypercoagulability.

36. Topic: Pathology – Cell injury
Question: The "point of no return" in irreversible cell injury is marked by:
(A) Cellular swelling
(B) Loss of membrane integrity and mitochondrial dysfunction
(C) Fatty change
(D) Increased protein synthesis
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Irreversible injury: severe mitochondrial damage, loss of plasma membrane integrity, and nuclear changes (pyknosis, karyorrhexis, karyolysis).

37. Topic: Pathology – Neoplasia
Question: A well‑differentiated tumor that grows slowly and does not metastasize is called:
(A) Anaplastic
(B) Malignant
(C) Benign
(D) Carcinoma in situ
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Benign tumors are well‑differentiated, non‑invasive, non‑metastatic; malignant tumors are poorly differentiated and invasive.

38. Topic: Pathology – Edema
Question: Cardiogenic edema is initially most evident in which body part?
(A) Periorbital region
(B) Dependent areas (feet/ankles)
(C) Sacrum
(D) Ascites
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Right heart failure causes systemic venous hypertension, leading to dependent pitting edema (feet/ankles in ambulatory patients).

39. Topic: Pathology – Shock
Question: In the early stage of hypovolemic shock, the compensatory mechanism that increases heart rate and vasoconstriction is mediated by:
(A) Parasympathetic activation
(B) Sympathetic nervous system and catecholamines
(C) Release of histamine
(D) Activation of the renin‑angiotensin system only
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Sympathetic activation (epinephrine, norepinephrine) increases HR, contractility, and vasoconstriction to maintain BP.

40. Topic: Pathology – Healing
Question: Which cell type is primarily responsible for wound contraction during secondary healing?
(A) Neutrophils
(B) Macrophages
(C) Myofibroblasts
(D) Endothelial cells
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Myofibroblasts (modified fibroblasts) contain actin and cause wound contraction; essential in secondary intention healing.

FOUNDATIONS OF NURSING (10)
41. Topic: Foundations – Hand hygiene
Question: The minimum duration for hand washing with soap and water (according to WHO) is:
(A) 10 seconds
(B) 20 seconds
(C) 40 seconds
(D) 60 seconds
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: WHO recommends 40‑60 seconds for hand washing with soap and water; alcohol rub takes 20‑30 seconds.

42. Topic: Foundations – Patient positioning
Question: A patient with orthopnea (difficulty breathing when lying flat) should be placed in which position?
(A) Supine
(B) Prone
(C) Semi‑Fowler's or high Fowler's
(D) Left lateral
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Fowler's position (head elevated 45‑90°) reduces venous return and allows diaphragm to descend, easing breathing.

43. Topic: Foundations – Medication administration
Question: When administering a subcutaneous injection of heparin, the nurse should:
(A) Aspirate before injecting
(B) Massage the site after injection
(C) Use a 1‑inch needle
(D) Not aspirate and do not massage
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Heparin is given subcutaneously without aspiration (to avoid hematoma) and without massage (prevents bruising).

44. Topic: Foundations – Ethics
Question: A nurse who acts in the best interest of the patient, even if not requested by the patient, is following the principle of:
(A) Autonomy
(B) Beneficence
(C) Non‑maleficence
(D) Justice
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Beneficence = doing good, acting for the patient's benefit. Non‑maleficence = "do no harm."

45. Topic: Foundations – Vital signs
Question: The normal respiratory rate for a healthy adult is:
(A) 8‑12 breaths/min
(B) 12‑20 breaths/min
(C) 20‑30 breaths/min
(D) 30‑40 breaths/min
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Normal adult respiratory rate is 12‑20 breaths/min; tachypnea >20, bradypnea <12.

46. Topic: Foundations – Nursing process
Question: The phase of the nursing process in which the nurse formulates a nursing diagnosis is:
(A) Assessment
(B) Diagnosis
(C) Planning
(D) Implementation
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: After assessment, the nurse analyzes data and formulates nursing diagnoses (NANDA) in the diagnosis phase.

47. Topic: Foundations – Isolation precautions
Question: A patient with active pulmonary tuberculosis requires which type of precautions?
(A) Contact
(B) Droplet
(C) Airborne
(D) Standard
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: TB requires airborne precautions (negative pressure room, N95 respirator). Droplet is for flu, pertussis.

48. Topic: Foundations – Legal aspects
Question: A nurse who threatens to restrain a patient without medical indication may be charged with:
(A) Negligence
(B) Malpractice
(C) Assault
(D) Battery
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Assault is the threat or attempt to touch a person without consent. Battery is actual harmful/offensive contact.

49. Topic: Foundations – Communication
Question: When a patient asks a direct question about a treatment, the nurse is unsure of the answer. The best response is:
(A) "I think it's fine."
(B) "Let me find out for you."
(C) "You should ask your doctor."
(D) "I don't know."
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Acknowledging uncertainty and promising to obtain correct information builds trust and ensures accuracy.

50. Topic: Foundations – Death and dying
Question: According to Kübler‑Ross, the stage where a patient tries to negotiate for more time is:
(A) Denial
(B) Anger
(C) Bargaining
(D) Depression
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Bargaining involves making deals (e.g., "If I live through this, I will…") to postpone death.

MEDICAL‑SURGICAL NURSING (15)
51. Topic: Med‑Surg – Diabetes insipidus
Question: Central diabetes insipidus is characterized by:
(A) High ADH, concentrated urine
(B) Low ADH, dilute urine
(C) High aldosterone, hypernatremia
(D) Low cortisol, hyponatremia
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Central DI: deficiency of ADH → large volume of dilute urine (specific gravity <1.005), polydipsia, hypernatremia.

52. Topic: Med‑Surg – Heart failure
Question: Which finding is an early sign of left‑sided heart failure?
(A) Peripheral edema
(B) Jugular venous distension
(C) Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (PND)
(D) Hepatomegaly
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Left heart failure → pulmonary congestion → PND, orthopnea, crackles. Right failure causes systemic congestion (edema, JVD).

53. Topic: Med‑Surg – Acute kidney injury
Question: In prerenal acute kidney injury, the most characteristic laboratory finding is:
(A) High urine sodium (>40 mEq/L)
(B) Fractional excretion of sodium (FeNa) <1%
(C) Urinary casts
(D) Hyperkalemia with normal urine output
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Prerenal AKI → low urine sodium (<20), FeNa <1% (due to sodium retention). Intrinsic renal → FeNa >2%.

54. Topic: Med‑Surg – COPD
Question: Which oxygen delivery device can deliver the highest concentration (up to 90%)?
(A) Nasal cannula 2 L/min
(B) Simple face mask
(C) Venturi mask
(D) Non‑rebreather mask
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Non‑rebreather with reservoir and one‑way valves can provide FiO2 up to 90% (10‑15 L/min). Venturi gives precise lower concentrations.

55. Topic: Med‑Surg – Seizure disorders
Question: During a generalized tonic‑clonic seizure, the nurse's priority action is to:
(A) Insert a tongue blade
(B) Restrain the patient
(C) Turn the patient to the side and protect the head
(D) Administer IV lorazepam immediately
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Protect from injury (cushion head, loosen tight clothing, turn to lateral position to prevent aspiration). Do not put anything in mouth.

56. Topic: Med‑Surg – Anaphylaxis
Question: The first sign of anaphylaxis is often:
(A) Hypotension
(B) Stridor
(C) Urticaria and flushing
(D) Cardiac arrest
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Cutaneous symptoms (hives, flushing, pruritus) are earliest. Respiratory and cardiovascular signs follow.

57. Topic: Med‑Surg – GI bleeding
Question: Melena (black, tarry stool) indicates bleeding from which site?
(A) Hemorrhoids
(B) Lower colon
(C) Upper GI tract (esophagus, stomach, duodenum)
(D) Small bowel only
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Blood from upper GI tract is digested by gastric acid and enzymes → black, tarry melena. Lower GI bleeding is bright red or maroon.

58. Topic: Med‑Surg – Chest tube
Question: Continuous bubbling in the water seal chamber of a chest drainage system indicates:
(A) Normal function
(B) Air leak
(C) Tension pneumothorax
(D) Tube obstruction
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Intermittent bubbling with expiration/Valsalva is normal; continuous bubbling suggests an air leak in the system or patient.

59. Topic: Med‑Surg – Thyroid disorders
Question: A patient with Graves' disease who develops fever, tachycardia, and delirium post‑operatively may be experiencing:
(A) Myxedema coma
(B) Thyroid storm
(C) Hypoparathyroidism
(D) Adrenal crisis
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Thyroid storm is a life‑threatening exacerbation of hyperthyroidism triggered by surgery, infection, etc. Myxedema coma is hypothyroid.

60. Topic: Med‑Surg – Electrolyte imbalance
Question: A serum potassium of 2.8 mEq/L is most likely to cause:
(A) Cardiac arrest in diastole
(B) Tall peaked T waves
(C) Muscle weakness and U waves on ECG
(D) Paralytic ileus and tetany
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Hypokalemia (<3.5) causes muscle weakness, U waves, flattened T waves, and risk of arrhythmia. Tetany is from hypocalcemia.

61. Topic: Med‑Surg – Stroke (CVA)
Question: In an ischemic stroke, recombinant tissue plasminogen activator (rtPA) should be given within how many hours of symptom onset?
(A) 1 hour
(B) 3 hours (up to 4.5)
(C) 6 hours
(D) 12 hours
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: rtPA is approved within 3 hours, but some guidelines extend to 4.5 hours. Beyond that, risk of hemorrhage outweighs benefit.

62. Topic: Med‑Surg – Diabetes mellitus
Question: A patient with type 1 diabetes who has nausea, vomiting, deep rapid breathing, and a fruity breath odor is likely in:
(A) Hypoglycemia
(B) Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)
(C) Hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state (HHS)
(D) Lactic acidosis
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: DKA presents with hyperglycemia, ketosis, acidosis (Kussmaul breathing), and acetone breath. HHS has no ketones.

63. Topic: Med‑Surg – Pneumonia
Question: Which finding is most specific for bacterial pneumonia?
(A) Dry cough
(B) Low‑grade fever
(C) Purulent sputum with lobar consolidation on CXR
(D) Wheezing
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Bacterial pneumonia often produces purulent (yellow/green) sputum and lobar consolidation. Viral/atypical may be diffuse.

64. Topic: Med‑Surg – Cirrhosis
Question: A patient with cirrhosis and ascites is at risk for spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP). The diagnostic paracentesis finding in SBP is:
(A) WBC count >250/mm3 with >50% neutrophils
(B) Low serum‑ascites albumin gradient
(C) Amylase >1000 U/L
(D) Red blood cells >10,000/mm3
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: SBP is diagnosed by ascitic fluid PMN >250 cells/mm3 without an intra‑abdominal source. High SAAG indicates portal hypertension.

65. Topic: Med‑Surg – Pain assessment
Question: Which pain scale is most appropriate for a cognitively impaired elderly patient?
(A) Numeric Rating Scale (0‑10)
(B) Visual Analog Scale
(C) FLACC scale
(D) PAINAD scale
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: PAINAD (Pain Assessment in Advanced Dementia) uses behaviors (breathing, vocalization, facial expression, consolability). FLACC is for children.

PEDIATRIC NURSING (8)
66. Topic: Pediatrics – Newborn reflexes
Question: The Moro (startle) reflex normally disappears by what age?
(A) 2 weeks
(B) 2 months
(C) 4‑6 months
(D) 12 months

Explanation: Moro reflex disappears by 4‑6 months; persistence beyond 6 months suggests neurological impairment.

67. Topic: Pediatrics – Dehydration
Question: The earliest clinical sign of dehydration in an infant is:
(A) Sunken fontanelle
(B) Tachycardia
(C) Decreased urine output
(D) Weight loss
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Tachycardia is an early compensatory sign. Sunken fontanelle, poor skin turgor, and oliguria occur with moderate‑severe dehydration.

68. Topic: Pediatrics – Immunization
Question: A child who received the first dose of MMR at 12 months should receive the second dose at:
(A) 15 months
(B) 18 months
(C) 4‑6 years (school entry)
(D) 10 years
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Two doses of MMR are required: first at 12‑15 months, second at 4‑6 years (or 1 month after first in outbreaks).

69. Topic: Pediatrics – Congenital heart disease
Question: In patent ductus arteriosus (PDA), the hallmark murmur is:
(A) Harsh holosystolic at left lower sternal border
(B) Continuous "machinery" murmur
(C) Ejection systolic at right upper sternal border
(D) Diastolic rumble at apex
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: PDA produces a continuous, "machinery‑like" murmur due to flow from aorta to pulmonary artery throughout the cardiac cycle.

70. Topic: Pediatrics – Croup
Question: A child with acute laryngotracheobronchitis (croup) has a characteristic:
(A) Barking cough and inspiratory stridor
(B) Wheezing and prolonged expiration
(C) Brassy cough with drooling
(D) Silent chest and absent breath sounds
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Croup (viral) presents with "seal‑like" barking cough, inspiratory stridor, and hoarseness. Drooling suggests epiglottitis.

71. Topic: Pediatrics – Kawasaki disease
Question: Which finding is a diagnostic criterion for Kawasaki disease?
(A) Strawberry tongue
(B) Petechial rash
(C) Symmetric arthritis
(D) Photophobia
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Kawasaki diagnostic criteria: fever >5 days, bilateral conjunctival injection, oral changes (strawberry tongue, fissured lips), rash, extremity changes, cervical lymphadenopathy.

72. Topic: Pediatrics – Child abuse
Question: The finding most suspicious for non‑accidental trauma in an infant is:
(A) Bruises on shins
(B) Fractured clavicle from birth
(C) Retinal hemorrhages and rib fractures of different ages
(D) Skull molding
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Retinal hemorrhages, posterior rib fractures, metaphyseal fractures, and multiple injuries at different healing stages are hallmarks of abuse.

73. Topic: Pediatrics – Milestones
Question: A 15‑month‑old child who cannot walk independently should be evaluated for:
(A) Normal variation
(B) Global developmental delay
(C) Muscular dystrophy
(D) Autism
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Most children walk by 12‑15 months; if not walking by 18 months, further evaluation is warranted. Delay may be global or specific.

PSYCHIATRIC NURSING (8)
74. Topic: Psychiatry – Mood disorders
Question: A patient with bipolar disorder in the manic phase is most likely to exhibit:
(A) Pressured speech and grandiosity
(B) Psychomotor retardation
(C) Anhedonia
(D) Hypersomnia
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Mania: elevated mood, grandiosity, decreased need for sleep, pressured speech, racing thoughts, risk‑taking behaviors.

75. Topic: Psychiatry – Obsessive‑compulsive disorder
Question: A patient repeatedly checks the door lock 20 times before leaving. This behavior is a(n):
(A) Compulsion
(B) Obsession
(C) Phobia
(D) Delusion
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Compulsions are repetitive behaviors performed to reduce anxiety from obsessions (recurrent intrusive thoughts). Checking is a compulsion.

76. Topic: Psychiatry – Schizophrenia
Question: "Word salad" (disorganized speech that is incomprehensible) is an example of:
(A) Alogia
(B) Thought blocking
(C) Tangentiality
(D) Incoherence
Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Incoherence (word salad) is severely disorganized speech lacking logical connection. Tangentiality eventually returns to topic; loosening of associations is related.

77. Topic: Psychiatry – Antidepressants
Question: The most serious adverse effect of MAO inhibitors is:
(A) Hypertensive crisis after eating tyramine‑rich foods
(B) Seizures
(C) Cardiotoxicity
(D) Weight loss
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: MAOIs block tyramine metabolism → ingestion of aged cheese, wine, etc. can cause severe hypertension and intracranial hemorrhage.

78. Topic: Psychiatry – Defense mechanisms
Question: A man who is angry at his boss but instead yells at his wife is using:
(A) Displacement
(B) Projection
(C) Reaction formation
(D) Sublimation
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Displacement redirects emotion from a threatening target to a safer one. Sublimation channels into socially acceptable activity.

79. Topic: Psychiatry – Anxiety disorders
Question: Panic disorder is characterized by:
(A) Recurrent, unexpected panic attacks and fear of future attacks
(B) Persistent worry about many events
(C) Fear of a specific object
(D) Repetitive behaviors
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Panic disorder: recurrent unexpected panic attacks with at least 1 month of concern about additional attacks. Agoraphobia may co‑occur.

80. Topic: Psychiatry – Personality disorders
Question: Which personality disorder is characterized by a pattern of instability in relationships, affect, and impulse control?
(A) Antisocial
(B) Borderline
(C) Narcissistic
(D) Paranoid
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Borderline personality disorder: emotional dysregulation, unstable relationships, impulsivity, fear of abandonment, self‑harm.

81. Topic: Psychiatry – Somatic symptom disorder
Question: A patient with multiple physical complaints without organic cause but genuine suffering is diagnosed with:
(A) Factitious disorder
(B) Malingering
(C) Somatic symptom disorder
(D) Conversion disorder
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Somatic symptom disorder: distress from physical symptoms (not intentionally produced) with disproportionate thoughts/feelings. Factitious = self‑induced for sick role.
